What It Removes

What the HPF-3 Filters Out

The HPF-3 targets the contaminants that Sydney Water's treatment process introduces into the supply.

Chlorine & Chloramines
Sydney Water treats supply with chlorine and chloramines. The HPF-3 carbon block removes both from every litre entering your home — eliminating the characteristic pool-water taste and odour from your taps and showers.
Sediment & Particulates
Sediment pre-filter removes particulates, rust and fine debris — particularly relevant in properties with older internal pipework. Protects appliances including hot water systems, washing machines and dishwashers from sediment accumulation.
Organic Compounds
The carbon block adsorbs organic compounds including disinfection byproducts (trihalomethanes and haloacetic acids) formed during the chlorination process. These are regulated under Australian guidelines but present at measurable levels in Sydney's supply.
Taste & Odour Compounds
Every tap in your home — kitchen, bathroom, laundry — delivers water that tastes and smells clean. Shower water is free from the chloramine odour that many Sydney households notice. Even garden and laundry water is filtered.

Note: The HPF-3 does not remove dissolved solids like fluoride or heavy metals. For maximum contaminant removal at the kitchen tap, Jean-Paul recommends combining a whole house system with an under-sink reverse osmosis system.

Who Should Install a Whole House Water Filter in Sydney?

A whole house water filter is the right choice for Sydney homeowners who want clean water throughout their entire property — not just at the kitchen sink. It's the most comprehensive domestic water filtration solution available, and it's most commonly chosen by:

  • Families with young children who want filtered water for baths, drinking, cooking and play — without managing multiple point-of-use systems
  • Health-conscious households who want the chloramine odour removed from showers and baths as well as drinking water
  • Property owners with older homes where internal pipework may introduce sediment and particulates that benefit from mains-level filtration
  • Households with skin sensitivities where chlorine and chloramines in shower and bath water are a concern
  • Appliance-focused homeowners who want to protect hot water systems, washing machines and dishwashers from sediment and chloramine damage over time

Whole House Filter vs Under Sink Filter — Which Do You Need?

A whole house filter and an under-sink filter serve different purposes and are not directly comparable. Here's the honest guide:

The HPF-3 whole house system ($3,150–$3,550) filters every outlet at the mains. The benefit is comprehensive coverage — your shower, bath, laundry and every kitchen and bathroom tap all receive filtered water. The limitation is that it doesn't remove dissolved contaminants like fluoride, heavy metals or nitrates — only chlorine, chloramines, sediment and organic compounds.

An under-sink filter or reverse osmosis system ($550–$1,740) treats only the kitchen drinking water but can achieve much deeper filtration at that single point — including fluoride removal (RO only) and removal of dissolved heavy metals, nitrates and microplastics.

Many of Jean-Paul's whole house customers also have an under-sink RO system at the kitchen for drinking water. This gives you the best of both worlds: clean water throughout the home for showering and laundry, plus purified water for drinking and cooking. Jean-Paul can install both in a single visit.

Does the HPF-3 Work for Sydney Apartments?

Whole house systems are best suited to freestanding houses and dual-occupancy properties where you have your own individual water meter that is accessible for installation. Most Sydney apartments share meter infrastructure at the building level, which makes a whole house installation impractical at the individual unit level.

If you're in an apartment, Jean-Paul recommends an under-sink filter or reverse osmosis system instead — which install cleanly under the kitchen sink without any mains access needed.

If you're unsure whether your property is suitable, call 0430 546 749. Jean-Paul will confirm within a minute whether the HPF-3 is right for your property type.
