Why Sydney Shower Water Affects Your Skin and Hair

Sydney Water treats the entire supply network with chloramines — a compound formed when chlorine reacts with ammonia. Chloramines serve as a long-lasting disinfectant that remains active throughout Sydney's extensive pipe network. They're effective at keeping water bacterially safe, but they come with a trade-off that many Sydney households notice: chloramines are harsher on skin and hair than chlorine alone, and they're much harder to remove.

When you shower in chloraminated water, you are exposing your skin and hair to a chemical disinfectant that:

  • Strips the natural oil barrier from skin, contributing to dryness, sensitivity and irritation
  • Disrupts the hair's cuticle layer, causing frizz, brittleness and reduced shine
  • Fades hair colour significantly faster than unfiltered water
  • Can aggravate eczema and other skin conditions, particularly in children
  • Produces a distinctive chlorine-like odour, especially in hot showers when steam intensifies the effect

Standard inline shower head filters containing vitamin C or KDF media can reduce free chlorine but are largely ineffective against the chloramines in Sydney's water supply. The only proven method for removing chloramines before they reach your shower is filtration at the water mains using a high-capacity catalytic carbon system — exactly what the HPF-3 whole house system provides.

The HPF-3 Whole House System — Filtered Water at Every Shower

The HPF-3 High Performance Filtration system is installed by Jean-Paul at your property's water mains — typically in the garage, laundry, or utility area. From that single installation point, every water outlet in the home receives filtered water: every shower, every bath, every kitchen tap, and every appliance connected to the cold water supply.

The HPF-3 uses a three-stage filtration process specifically designed for Sydney water conditions:

  • Stage 1 — 5-micron sediment pre-filter: Removes suspended particles, rust, and silt that can cause premature wear on downstream filter media
  • Stage 2 — Catalytic carbon block: This is the critical stage for Sydney households — catalytic carbon is one of the few media effective at reducing chloramines, along with standard chlorine, taste and odour
  • Stage 3 — 1-micron post-filter: Final polishing stage that captures any remaining fine particles and microplastics

The HPF-3 is engineered for high-flow applications — it maintains full household pressure even with multiple showers running simultaneously. Installing a whole house system in an apartment is generally not feasible — shared building water infrastructure and strata configurations prevent mains-level installation in most cases.
