When to Choose Reverse Osmosis

A reverse osmosis system is the right choice when your primary goal is the highest possible quality drinking and cooking water. If you want water that is genuinely free of fluoride, chloramines, heavy metals, nitrates, PFAS and dissolved contaminants — water that is objectively cleaner than what comes out of any Sydney tap — reverse osmosis delivers that at the most accessible price point.

Choose RO if:

  • You live in an apartment and a whole house system is not feasible
  • Fluoride removal is a priority — for young children, baby formula, or personal preference
  • You want to eliminate the taste and odour of chloramines from your drinking water
  • Your budget is under $1,500 installed
  • You're renting and need a non-invasive system that can be removed when you move
  • You're starting out and want the highest-impact single upgrade for your health

Jean-Paul's most popular RO system is the Pure Plus+ 5-Stage Reverse Osmosis at $840 installed — it removes up to 99% of dissolved contaminants and is the system most of his customers choose as their first filtration upgrade.

When to Choose the HPF-3 Whole House Filter

The whole house HPF-3 filter is the right choice when you want filtered water throughout the entire home — not just at the kitchen tap. If showering in chlorinated water is affecting your skin, hair or scalp; if you want to protect your appliances and hot water system; or if you simply want comprehensive coverage across every water outlet in the property, the HPF-3 is the system to install.

Choose the whole house HPF-3 if:

  • You own a house and want filtered water at every shower, bath and tap
  • Skin or hair quality is a concern — chloramine removal from shower water makes a noticeable difference
  • You want to protect your hot water system, washing machine and dishwasher
  • You have young children who bathe regularly in tap water
  • Your property has older pipes and you want sediment removed at the mains
  • You want the most comprehensive whole-property solution available

The HPF-3 is priced from $3,150 installed. See the full details at the Whole House Water Filter and High Performance Filtration pages.

Can You Have Both? — The Combined Approach

Yes, and for Sydney homeowners who want the most complete protection, having both systems installed is the answer Jean-Paul recommends most often. The combination works like this:

  • The HPF-3 whole house system filters all mains water — removing chlorine, chloramines, sediment and microplastics before they reach any shower, bath, tap or appliance in the home
  • The reverse osmosis system under the kitchen sink then takes the already-filtered water and purifies it further — removing fluoride, any residual dissolved contaminants, and producing water of the highest possible drinking quality

This combined approach addresses every concern simultaneously: shower water quality, skin and hair, appliance protection, drinking water purity, and fluoride removal. Jean-Paul has installed this combination in many Sydney homes and can quote both systems together. The combined cost starts from approximately $3,990 for the HPF-3 plus Pure Essential under-sink system.

Property Types — Which System Suits Your Home?

Apartments and units: Reverse osmosis is the primary option. Under-sink systems install in the kitchen cupboard without mains access or strata approval in most cases. Jean-Paul installs these across all Sydney apartment types. The Pure Plus+ 5-stage RO at $840 is the most popular choice for apartment residents.

Freestanding houses: Both options are available. Older homes with established pipework benefit particularly from the whole house system's sediment and microplastics removal. Many house owners choose to start with an RO system for drinking water, then add the whole house filter when budget allows.

Townhouses and semi-detached: Generally suitable for both systems, depending on plumbing configuration. Jean-Paul will assess the specific property before quoting.

Rental properties: Under-sink and reverse osmosis systems are the practical option for renters — they connect to the cold water supply under the sink and can be removed and reinstalled when you move. Jean-Paul has an installer-friendly approach to rental properties. See the dedicated water filter for renters guide.
