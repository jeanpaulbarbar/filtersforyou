Why the Choice Between Whole House and Under Sink Matters for Sydney Homeowners

Sydney's tap water is treated to a standard that is safe to drink — but safe and great are two very different things. Sydney Water uses chlorine and chloramines to disinfect the supply, chemicals that are effective at killing pathogens but that affect taste, smell and, over time, your body. Older building pipes add sediment, dissolved metals and further contaminants on top of what is already in the treated water.

The choice of whole house versus under sink is really a question of what you want to filter and where. Choosing the right system means your family gets the benefits they actually need at a price that makes sense. Choosing the wrong one means either overspending on filtration you will rarely use, or under-investing and wondering why your water still tastes off.

Jean-Paul at Filters For You has helped Sydney households across the Inner West, Eastern Suburbs, North Shore, Western Sydney and beyond make this decision. Here is how it breaks down in plain language.

What Whole House Water Filtration Does

The HPF-3 High Performance Whole House Filtration System is installed at your water meter or mains entry point — before water reaches any tap, shower, bath or appliance in your home. That means everything running through your home's plumbing is filtered before you ever touch it.

The HPF-3 uses a high-capacity carbon filtration media to remove:

  • Chlorine and chloramines — the disinfectants used in Sydney's water supply
  • Sediment and particulate matter
  • Microplastics, which are increasingly found in Australian urban water supplies
  • Taste and odour compounds that make tap water unpleasant
  • Some heavy metals, depending on source and pipe condition

Every tap in the house delivers filtered water. Every shower. Every bath your children take. The water your dishwasher uses. The water that fills your kettle. All of it filtered at the source before it ever enters your home's pipework.

This matters more than most people initially realise. Research consistently shows that a significant proportion of daily chlorine exposure comes through the skin and respiratory system during a hot shower — not through drinking. Whole house filtration is the only way to address that. For families with young children, people with eczema or sensitive skin, or health-conscious households who want comprehensive protection, whole house filtration is often the deciding factor.

The HPF-3 is installed outside at the side of your home near the water meter, typically taking 2–3 hours. Jean-Paul uses WaterMark certified components and backs every installation with a lifetime workmanship warranty. Pricing starts at $3,150 depending on site conditions.

What Under Sink Water Filtration Does

Under sink systems sit inside your kitchen cabinet and connect directly to your cold water line. They deliver filtered water through a dedicated separate tap on your sink or benchtop. They filter the water you drink and cook with — not your showers or laundry.

Filters For You offers a full range of under sink systems, from the entry-level Pure Essential twin stage carbon filter through to the Pure Luxe smart-monitoring reverse osmosis system. The core products are:

  • Pure Essential ($550) — twin stage carbon filtration. Excellent for chlorine, taste and odour. The ideal starting point for apartments, renters and budget-conscious buyers.
  • Pure Plus+ ($840) — 5 stage reverse osmosis. Removes fluoride, heavy metals, dissolved solids, bacteria and PFAS. The most popular drinking water system Jean-Paul installs.
  • Pure Compact ($930) — compact 3 stage alkaline RO for smaller under-sink spaces.
  • Pure Premium ($1,180) — 7 stage RO with alkaline remineralisation for the most refined drinking experience available.
  • Pure Advanced ($1,281) — quick-change alkaline RO for households who prefer easy DIY cartridge replacement.

Under sink systems are ideal for apartments, rental properties, and any home where the primary concern is the quality of drinking and cooking water rather than shower and bath water. They are compact and hidden beneath your sink — and they can be removed and taken with you when you move.

When to Choose Whole House Filtration

Whole house filtration is the right choice when you own a freestanding Sydney home with accessible mains plumbing, and when you want filtered water throughout the entire property — not only at the kitchen tap.

Choose the HPF-3 if any of the following apply:

  • You have young children who bathe and shower daily
  • You or a family member has sensitive skin, eczema or respiratory sensitivities
  • You want to protect appliances — dishwashers, washing machines and hot water systems — from sediment and chloramine damage
  • You want every tap in the house delivering clean, great-tasting water — not just the kitchen
  • You are a health-focused household that values comprehensive water protection
  • You are planning a renovation and want to install the system during the build phase

The HPF-3 is the flagship product at Filters For You and Jean-Paul's most-recommended system for freestanding houses in Sydney. It is a significant investment, but it delivers the most comprehensive water quality protection available for a Sydney home.

When to Choose Under Sink or Reverse Osmosis

Under sink systems are the right choice for apartments, rental properties, and homes where the primary concern is the quality of drinking and cooking water rather than the rest of the home's water supply.

Choose under sink or RO if:

  • You live in an apartment or unit where mains access is shared or controlled by strata
  • You are renting and want a portable system you can take with you when you move
  • Your current budget is $550–$1,281 and you want a professional installation
  • Fluoride removal is important to you — this requires a reverse osmosis membrane
  • Your goal is to stop buying bottled water and switch to home-filtered drinking water
  • You are already reasonably satisfied with shower water quality and mainly want better drinking water

Can You Have Both? The Ultimate Sydney Home Setup

Yes — and a growing number of Sydney homeowners are choosing exactly this approach. The HPF-3 whole house system at the mains removes chlorine, chloramines and sediment from everything in the house, while a Pure Plus+ or Pure Premium reverse osmosis system under the kitchen sink adds a second level of filtration for drinking and cooking water. This adds fluoride removal, heavy metal removal and ultrafine filtration down to 0.0001 micron for what goes in your body.

The combination gives you the best of both worlds: clean, chemical-free water at every tap and shower throughout the house, plus hospital-grade purity for every glass of water, every cup of tea and every meal. Jean-Paul has installed this dual setup in many Sydney homes and can quote both systems as a package.

Sydney-Specific Water Quality Considerations

Sydney's water supply uses chloramines rather than plain chlorine in many distribution zones. Chloramines are harder to remove than standard chlorine — they require a higher-quality activated carbon media and longer contact time within the filter housing. The HPF-3 is specifically designed to handle chloramine-heavy supplies effectively. Many cheaper hardware store whole house filters are not designed to manage chloramines adequately, which is one reason why the professional-grade systems installed by Filters For You consistently outperform off-the-shelf alternatives.

Older Sydney suburbs — particularly parts of the Inner West, Eastern Suburbs and heritage areas of the North Shore — often have older copper or galvanised steel plumbing that can introduce additional sediment and metallic taste into the water before it ever reaches your tap. Whole house sediment filtration at the mains helps significantly, but a reverse osmosis system under the kitchen sink remains the most reliable way to address dissolved metals in ageing pipe systems.
