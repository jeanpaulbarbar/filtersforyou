Common Water Filter Problems Explained

Slow flow or low pressure from filtered tap

The most common complaint Jean-Paul receives. In most cases this is caused by one or more of: an overdue cartridge restricting flow, a low-capacity storage tank on an RO system, low incoming water pressure, or a partially closed supply valve. Jean-Paul will check all four on the initial visit.

Water tastes or smells odd after filtering

A carbon cartridge past its service life will stop adsorbing chlorine and other compounds — and can actually release trapped contaminants back into the water. If your filtered water has developed a chlorine, musty, or chemical taste, the cartridges almost certainly need replacing. RO membranes can also become fouled over time, causing taste changes.

System is leaking

Push-fit fittings (John Guest or similar) are used throughout most under-sink systems. These can work loose over time, especially if the cabinet is accessed frequently. A failed O-ring inside a filter housing is another common cause. Jean-Paul carries common fittings and O-rings and can resolve most leaks without ordering parts.

RO system running continuously or won't shut off

This usually indicates a failed auto-shutoff valve (ASO valve) or a storage tank that has lost its air charge. Both are repairable on-site. Ignoring this fault wastes significant water and can stress the RO membrane.

System making noise

A gurgling or rushing sound is normal when an RO system is filling its storage tank. Persistent noise between fill cycles can indicate a failed check valve or a tank pressure issue. Jean-Paul will identify the cause and advise on the fix.
