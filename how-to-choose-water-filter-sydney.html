Decision Framework

5 Steps to Choosing the Right Water Filter

Answer these five questions in order and you'll know exactly which system is right for your Sydney home — before you speak to anyone.

1

What type of property do you live in?

Apartment or unit: Your options are under-sink and reverse osmosis systems — these install under your kitchen sink and do not require access to building mains or strata approval in most cases. Start with Step 2 for budget guidance.

Freestanding house or townhouse: You have access to all system types — under-sink, reverse osmosis, or the HPF-3 whole house filter. Continue to Step 2 to determine which is right for you.

Renting: Under-sink and reverse osmosis systems are the practical option. They can be installed and removed without permanent changes to the property. See the water filter guide for renters for more detail.

2

What is your primary goal?

Pure drinking water (removing fluoride, chloramines, heavy metals, PFAS): You need a reverse osmosis system. Only RO membranes remove fluoride effectively. The Pure Plus+ 5-Stage RO at $840 installed is the most popular and recommended starting point.

Filtered shower water, skin and hair improvement, whole-home coverage: You need the HPF-3 Whole House Filter ($3,150–$3,550). It treats all water at the mains — every shower, bath, tap and appliance.

Both — purified drinking water AND filtered shower water: Install both. Jean-Paul installs this combination in many Sydney homes. The combined cost starts from approximately $3,990 installed.

3

What contaminants do you most want to remove?

Fluoride: Reverse osmosis only. Standard carbon filters and whole house systems do not remove fluoride. Any RO system in the Filters For You range removes fluoride at 93–99% efficiency.

Chlorine and chloramines (taste, odour, skin): Both systems remove these — RO comprehensively at the drinking tap, whole house across every outlet in the home.

Heavy metals (lead, arsenic, mercury): Reverse osmosis is the most effective solution — the RO membrane blocks dissolved metal ions that carbon filters cannot capture.

PFAS (forever chemicals): Reverse osmosis membranes are one of the few proven methods for reducing PFAS in drinking water. Whole house carbon systems have limited effectiveness against PFAS.

Sediment and microplastics: Both systems remove these. The whole house system filters them out before they reach any outlet; RO systems have a dedicated sediment pre-filter stage.

Bacteria: Reverse osmosis effectively removes bacteria at the drinking tap. If you have concerns about bacterial contamination in your water supply, speak to Jean-Paul about the correct system for your situation.

4

What is your budget?

Under $700: Pure Essential twin-stage under-sink filter — $550 installed. Removes chlorine, sediment, taste and odour. Best-value entry-level option. Annual service $340/yr.

$700 – $1,000: Pure Plus+ 5-Stage Reverse Osmosis — $840 installed. Removes 99% of dissolved contaminants including fluoride, chloramines and PFAS. Jean-Paul's most recommended first system. Annual service $420/yr.

$1,000 – $1,500: Pure Compact ($930.88), Pure Premium ($1,180) or Pure Advanced ($1,281.88) — progressively more advanced RO systems with additional stages and features including alkaline remineralisation and quick-change cartridges.

$3,000 – $4,000: HPF-3 Whole House Filter — $3,150 to $3,550 installed. The flagship whole-property solution. Annual service $550/yr.

$4,000+: HPF-3 whole house plus RO under-sink combination — the most comprehensive solution available for Sydney homes.

5

How large is your household and how much filtered water do you consume?

1–2 people: The Pure Essential or Pure Plus+ RO is more than adequate for drinking and cooking needs. The Pure Plus+ is still recommended for its fluoride removal capability.

3–5 people (family): The Pure Plus+ or Pure Premium RO systems handle family-sized daily consumption comfortably. The tank refills continuously so supply is never an issue.

Large families or high consumption: The Pure Advanced quick-change system with its efficient membrane is ideal for larger households. The whole house HPF-3 system is also an excellent foundation for families, with the RO system added for drinking water.

Young children present: Jean-Paul prioritises an RO system for families with babies or young children — fluoride removal for formula preparation and the removal of all dissolved contaminants from drinking water are the key considerations.

What Sydney Water Actually Contains — and Why It Matters for Your Choice

Understanding what is in Sydney's tap water helps you understand what you need to filter. Sydney Water treats all supply with the following:

  • Chloramines: A compound of chlorine and ammonia used as the primary disinfectant. More persistent than chlorine and harder to remove. Associated with taste, odour, skin dryness and hair quality issues. Removed by both carbon block under-sink filters and RO systems. Not effectively removed by standard inline shower head filters.
  • Fluoride: Added at the target level of 1.0mg/L — the highest of any Australian capital city. Removed only by reverse osmosis (93–99% efficiency). Relevant particularly for families preparing infant formula and parents of young children.
  • Disinfection byproducts (DBPs): Including trihalomethanes and haloacetic acids formed during chlorination. Regulated but present. Removed by RO systems.
  • PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances): Detected in some Sydney catchments at very low levels. Reverse osmosis is one of the few treatment methods effective against PFAS. Relevant for households with heightened health concerns.
  • Microplastics: Detected in urban water supplies including Sydney. Both under-sink carbon filters and whole house systems remove microplastics via mechanical filtration.
  • Sediment: Small particles, rust and silt. More prevalent in older buildings and properties with ageing pipework. Removed by all filtration systems.

Do I Need a Licensed Plumber to Install a Water Filter in Sydney?

Yes. Under the Plumbing Code of Australia and NSW plumbing legislation, any water filter that connects to the property's water supply plumbing must be installed by a licensed plumber. This applies to all under-sink, reverse osmosis and whole house filtration systems.

DIY installation of plumbed water filters in NSW is illegal and risks voiding your home and contents insurance policy. It also creates liability issues if water damage occurs at or near the installation.

Apartment vs House — Key Differences for Water Filter Selection

Apartments: The water mains for an apartment building are shared infrastructure managed by the building owner or body corporate. Individual apartment owners cannot install a whole house system at the building's main water supply. The correct approach is an under-sink or reverse osmosis system that connects to the cold water supply line under your kitchen sink — inside your apartment, within your own plumbing. No mains access, no building approval required in most cases. Jean-Paul installs these in apartments throughout Sydney with zero structural changes to the property.

Houses: Whole house systems install at the external water meter or within the garage/utility area at the point where the mains water enters the property. This gives Jean-Paul a clean installation point before the water reaches any outlet in the home. Houses with older galvanised or copper pipes particularly benefit from the whole house system's sediment removal, which reduces particulate accumulation throughout the pipework.

Townhouses and semis: These vary. Some have individual mains access suitable for whole house installation; others share mains infrastructure with adjacent properties. Jean-Paul will confirm suitability during a free assessment or phone call before any booking is made.
