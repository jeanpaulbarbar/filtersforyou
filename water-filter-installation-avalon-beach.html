Water Filtration in Avalon Beach, What Northern Beaches Families Need to Know

Avalon Beach residents who want better water at home increasingly turn to dedicated filtration, and the reasons hold across detached homes, townhouses, units and apartments. Cleaner drinking water, healthier showers, longer-lasting appliances and a smaller plastic-bottle footprint over the years.

Sydney's tap water contains chlorine and chloramines added during treatment, low levels of fluoride, trace sediment and microplastics that accumulate in older pipes and reservoirs. While Sydney Water meets Australian drinking water guidelines, many Avalon Beach families want a higher standard, one that removes these compounds entirely rather than simply keeping them below regulatory thresholds. That is what a well-specified water filtration system delivers.

What Water Contaminants Does a Filter Remove in Avalon Beach?

Sydney Water's treatment process uses chlorine and chloramines to disinfect the water supply. These disinfectants are effective at killing pathogens, but they remain present in household tap water and can affect taste, smell and, for some people, skin health when used in the shower. In addition to disinfectants, tap water in Sydney can contain:

  • Sediment from ageing pipes and reservoirs
  • Microplastics from infrastructure and environmental sources
  • Fluoride at low concentrations (added intentionally for dental health)
  • Trace heavy metals including lead, particularly in older plumbing systems
  • PFAS compounds from industrial and environmental contamination

A two-stage under-sink filter removes chlorine and sediment. A five or seven-stage reverse osmosis system removes all of the above, including fluoride, heavy metals and PFAS, delivering water that exceeds Australian drinking standards by a significant margin.
