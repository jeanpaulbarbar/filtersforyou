Water Filtration in Miranda — What Sutherland Shire Families Need to Know

Miranda is one of Sydney's most family-centric suburbs. The streets around Westfield Miranda, along the Kingsway corridor and through the quieter residential pockets toward Gymea and Caringbah are lined with detached houses occupied by families who care deeply about their health, their home's long-term value and the quality of what their children eat and drink every day.

Sydney's tap water contains chlorine and chloramines added during treatment, low levels of fluoride, trace sediment and microplastics that accumulate in older pipes and reservoirs. While Sydney Water meets Australian drinking water guidelines, many Miranda families want a higher standard — one that removes these compounds entirely rather than simply keeping them below regulatory thresholds. That is what a well-specified water filtration system delivers.

What Water Contaminants Does a Filter Remove in Miranda?

Sydney Water's treatment process uses chlorine and chloramines to disinfect the water supply. These disinfectants are effective at killing pathogens, but they remain present in household tap water and can affect taste, smell and — for some people — skin health when used in the shower. In addition to disinfectants, tap water in Sydney can contain:

  • Sediment from ageing pipes and reservoirs
  • Microplastics from infrastructure and environmental sources
  • Fluoride at low concentrations (added intentionally for dental health)
  • Trace heavy metals including lead, particularly in older plumbing systems
  • PFAS compounds from industrial and environmental contamination

A two-stage under-sink filter removes chlorine and sediment. A five or seven-stage reverse osmosis system removes all of the above, including fluoride, heavy metals and PFAS, delivering water that exceeds Australian drinking standards by a significant margin.
