What Makes a Water Filter "Best" for Sydney?

The best water filter for any Sydney home is the one that matches your actual priorities, property type and budget. There is no universal answer — but there are clear patterns for different types of households. Here's how to think through it.

Priority 1: Taste and Odour Only

If your main concern is the chloramine taste and odour in Sydney tap water — that slightly chemical smell you notice when filling a glass or making tea — a standard under-sink carbon block filter delivers a significant, immediately noticeable improvement. The Pure Essential twin-stage system at $550 installed is the right choice: it removes chlorine, chloramines (partially), sediment and the compounds most responsible for taste issues. Annual maintenance is $80–$120. It's clean, hidden under the sink, and does the job well for taste-focused households.

Priority 2: Maximum Contaminant Removal

If you want to remove as many contaminants as possible — including fluoride, heavy metals, nitrates and microplastics that a carbon filter cannot address — a reverse osmosis system is the definitive choice. The Pure Plus+ 5-stage RO at $840 removes 99% of dissolved contaminants. It's the most popular system Jean-Paul installs and the one he would recommend to most Sydney families with young children or health goals. For the 7-stage version with alkaline remineralisation and maximum contaminant removal, the Pure Premium at $1,180 covers everything.

Priority 3: Whole Home Coverage

Some Sydney households want clean water at every point in the home — not just the kitchen tap. This is particularly common for families with young children (clean bath and shower water) or households with skin sensitivities (chloramine in shower water). The HPF-3 whole house system at $3,150 addresses this by filtering at the mains. It works best in freestanding houses with accessible water meters — ideal for suburbs like Concord, Burwood, Homebush and Strathfield.

The Most Important Thing: Licensed Installation

Whatever system you choose, the installation matters as much as the product. An under-sink or whole house filter that is incorrectly installed can leak, void your home insurance, and in some cases introduce bacteria if non-food-grade fittings are used. Jean-Paul holds NSW Plumbing Licence 461511C and installs every system to code. His lifetime workmanship warranty means if anything is ever wrong with the installation, he returns and fixes it free of charge.

Best Water Filters by Sydney Suburb Type

Different Sydney property types lend themselves to different systems:

  • Inner west terrace houses (Newtown, Leichhardt, Marrickville, Petersham): Under-sink RO is ideal. Compact under-sink space, limited mains access makes whole house impractical for most. Jean-Paul is based 5km from most inner west suburbs.
  • Mid-western family homes (Burwood, Strathfield, Homebush, Concord): HPF-3 whole house system is a strong option given accessible meters and larger homes. Many also add an under-sink RO for drinking water.
  • Apartments: Under-sink or RO — both ideal, no strata approval needed in most cases. Whole house not viable.
  • Rentals: Under-sink systems are fully removable when you move. Jean-Paul can advise on the right approach for your lease situation.
