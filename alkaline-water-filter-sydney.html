What Is an Alkaline Water Filter — and Do You Actually Need One?

The term "alkaline water filter" is used loosely across the market, covering everything from simple mineral sachets to multi-stage reverse osmosis systems. Understanding the difference matters, particularly if you are spending money on a system for your Sydney home.

Pure water has a neutral pH of 7.0. Sydney's mains water typically sits between 7.0 and 8.0 depending on the source reservoir and treatment batch, but the addition of chloramines and fluoride — both acidic compounds — can bring the effective drinking pH slightly lower. Alkaline water refers to water with a pH above 7.0, ideally in the 7.5 to 9.0 range, achieved through the addition of beneficial minerals rather than chemical manipulation.

The most compelling reason to choose an alkaline water system is not the pH alone — it is the mineral content. Calcium and magnesium added by an alkaline remineralisation cartridge contribute to daily mineral intake, improve water taste significantly, and give the water a smooth, rounded quality that plain reverse osmosis (which can taste slightly flat due to the absence of minerals) does not provide.

How Alkaline RO Systems Work — Stage by Stage

Filters For You installs reverse osmosis systems with a dedicated alkaline remineralisation post-filter. This two-part process is the reason these systems outperform standalone ionisers and simple alkaline filter pitchers.

Stage 1: Contaminant Removal via RO Membrane

The reverse osmosis membrane filters water at 0.0001 micron — one ten-thousandth of a micron. At this scale, virtually everything dissolved in the water is blocked and flushed to drain, including:

  • Chlorine and chloramines (added by Sydney Water for disinfection)
  • Fluoride — removed at 93 to 96% efficiency, the highest of any filter type
  • PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances), including PFOA and PFOS
  • Lead, mercury and other heavy metals
  • Microplastics and suspended sediment
  • Bacteria and most viruses (in systems with appropriate membrane ratings)
  • Nitrates and dissolved salts

After the RO stage, the water is essentially pure — but it is also slightly acidic (pH around 5.5 to 6.5) and stripped of minerals. This is exactly what the alkaline stage corrects.

Stage 2: Alkaline Remineralisation

The alkaline post-filter — typically a ceramic ball or mineralisation cartridge — adds calcium and magnesium ions back into the purified water as it passes through. This raises the pH naturally to between 7.5 and 9.0 and gives the water a pleasant, smooth mineral taste. No electricity is involved, no chemicals are added, and no acidic waste stream is created.

The result is water that is simultaneously free of contaminants and rich in beneficial minerals. This is what distinguishes alkaline RO from a water ioniser, which raises pH electrochemically but does nothing to remove dissolved contaminants from the incoming supply.

Why Sydney Homes Benefit Most from Alkaline RO

Sydney Water supplies some of the highest-quality treated municipal water in Australia, but it comes with characteristics that concern health-conscious households. Sydney Water adds chloramines for disinfection (a combination of chlorine and ammonia that is harder to remove than plain chlorine), fluoride at a target of 1.0mg/L (the highest fluoride dosing of any Australian capital), and residual levels of PFAS have been identified in certain Sydney catchment areas.

Plain carbon filters reduce chloramine taste and odour but do not remove fluoride or PFAS. Only reverse osmosis with a matched membrane achieves meaningful removal of all three. Adding an alkaline remineralisation stage to the RO process corrects the slight acidity of post-RO water and restores the smooth mineral taste that most households prefer.

The combination — pure water, elevated pH, natural minerals — is why the Pure Compact, Pure Advanced and Pure Premium have become the most frequently requested systems Filters For You installs for health-conscious Sydney families.
