System Types

An under-sink carbon filter (like the Pure Essential, $550) removes chlorine, sediment, taste and odour effectively. It has a fast flow rate and low annual maintenance cost — the right choice if your primary concern is taste improvement and basic filtration. A reverse osmosis system (from $840) goes further — using a semi-permeable membrane to remove up to 99% of dissolved contaminants including chloramines, fluoride, heavy metals, nitrates and microplastics. The water is stored in a small tank and dispensed from a dedicated tap. If you want the absolute best drinking water quality comparable to premium bottled water, reverse osmosis is the choice.
A whole house filter (the HPF-3 system, from $3,150) connects at your home's water meter — before the water enters any part of the house. Every tap, shower, bath, dishwasher, washing machine and garden tap receives filtered water. It's the most comprehensive option, ensuring filtered water is available everywhere in the home, not just from the kitchen tap. It's suitable for freestanding houses with accessible water meters. Jean-Paul confirms suitability before booking.

Water Quality

Sydney Water's supply meets Australian Drinking Water Guidelines and is considered safe by regulatory standards. However, it contains chloramines (which affect taste and odour), fluoride (added for dental health), disinfection byproducts including trihalomethanes, and trace microplastics. Many Sydney residents choose filtration not because the water is unsafe, but because they want to remove these additives and improve the taste, smell and overall quality of their daily drinking water.
Filters For You systems remove: chlorine and chloramines (taste and odour), sediment and particulates, microplastics, fluoride (RO systems), heavy metals including lead, disinfection byproducts, nitrates and nitrites (RO systems), bacteria (RO systems with appropriate membranes), and PFAS compounds (RO systems). The level of removal depends on the system stage count and membrane type. Jean-Paul will advise on which system addresses your specific concerns.
Microplastics have been detected in urban water supplies globally, including Sydney. Standard carbon filters can capture larger microplastic particles; reverse osmosis membranes remove essentially all microplastic particles due to the extremely small pore size (typically 0.0001 microns). Regarding household pipes — modern copper and PVC pipes do not add significant microplastics. Older plastic piping materials in some buildings can contribute trace plasticisers, but the primary microplastic exposure in tap water comes from the supply network. An RO system installed at the point of use effectively addresses microplastics from all sources before the water reaches your glass.
Sydney Water's treated supply has not been found to contain PFAS at levels exceeding health guidelines. However, some areas of Sydney — particularly around Botany Bay, certain defence sites and industrial zones — have identified PFAS in groundwater. Sydney Water's treated distribution system is separate from local groundwater. For residents in areas with known groundwater PFAS monitoring who want maximum assurance, a reverse osmosis system is the recommended choice — RO is currently the most effective residential technology for PFAS removal from drinking water.

Maintenance & Warranty

For a standard twin-stage under-sink filter, cartridges should be replaced every 12 months. For reverse osmosis systems, sediment and carbon pre-filters are replaced every 12 months; the RO membrane is replaced every 2–3 years; post-filters are replaced every 12 months. Jean-Paul provides a full maintenance schedule at the end of every installation. Filters For You stocks replacement cartridges for all systems.
The lifetime workmanship warranty covers all labour completed by Jean-Paul during the installation — the connections, fittings, tap mounting and any modifications made to your plumbing during the job. If any aspect of Jean-Paul's installation work develops a fault, he will return and rectify it at no charge. The warranty covers workmanship, not system components themselves (which are covered by the manufacturer's warranty). This warranty applies for as long as you own the property.
The most reliable indicator is the annual calendar — replace cartridges every 12 months regardless of whether you notice a change in taste. Other signs include a reduction in flow rate from the filter tap (indicating a clogged pre-filter), a return of chlorine taste or odour (indicating an expired carbon stage), or a warning notification from the app (for Pure Luxe smart monitoring systems). Jean-Paul can set up annual servicing reminders at the time of installation.
