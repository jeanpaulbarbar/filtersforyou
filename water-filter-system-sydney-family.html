Why Sydney Families Are Prioritising Water Filtration

Water filtration is no longer a niche concern for Sydney families — it is fast becoming an expected part of a healthy home setup. Greater Sydney's water supply, while meeting Australian drinking water standards, uses chloramines as a primary disinfectant across most distribution zones. Chloramines are more persistent than plain chlorine and more difficult to remove, with a distinct taste and odour that many families find unpleasant in their drinking water.

Beyond chloramines, Sydney's ageing pipe network — particularly in older suburbs — introduces sediment, microplastics and trace heavy metals into the water before it reaches your kitchen tap. Families with young children, those with eczema-prone skin, and households that prioritise a clean diet are increasingly investing in water filtration as a straightforward, one-time step toward better health outcomes for their household.

Jean-Paul at Filters For You installs water filtration systems for families across Greater Sydney. His approach is simple: understand your household's water usage, priorities and budget, recommend the right system without upselling on the day, and install it neatly and professionally so it performs as it should for years to come.

What Your Family Actually Needs from a Water Filter

Not every family needs the same system. Jean-Paul considers the following before recommending a water filtration solution:

  • Household size: A family of five has significantly different water usage from a couple. Higher usage households benefit from systems with larger capacity tanks or higher-capacity cartridges.
  • Young children: Families with babies and toddlers often prioritise removing fluoride and heavy metals from the water used for formula preparation and cooking. A reverse osmosis system like the Pure Plus+ is the most thorough solution for this purpose.
  • Whole house vs point-of-use: Under sink systems filter only the connected tap. If your priority is drinking and cooking water, an under sink RO system is the best value. If you want chlorine removed from shower and bath water — particularly for children with sensitive skin or eczema — the HPF-3 whole house system is the right choice.
  • Kitchen space: Some Sydney homes have limited under-sink space. Jean-Paul assesses this at booking and recommends the Pure Compact or Pure Advanced if space is a constraint.
  • Maintenance preference: Families who want the simplest possible maintenance often choose the Pure Advanced quick-change range, where annual cartridge replacements take less than 10 minutes without any tools.

Under Sink Systems for Sydney Families

An under sink water filter is the most popular starting point for Sydney families. It installs neatly in the cabinet under your kitchen sink, connects to a dedicated filtered tap on your benchtop, and provides clean water for drinking, cooking, and filling water bottles — on demand, unlimited, at a fraction of the cost of bottled water.

The entry point for a professionally installed under sink system is the Pure Essential at $550.88 — twin stage carbon filtration removing chlorine, chloramines and sediment. For families who want to go further and remove fluoride, heavy metals and microplastics, the Pure Plus+ 5 stage reverse osmosis at $840 is Jean-Paul's most-recommended system for Sydney households.

For families who want the best possible water quality — particularly those with young children or health-focused households — the Pure Premium 7 stage RO at $1,180 adds an alkaline remineralisation stage that produces structured, mineral-rich water with a genuinely premium taste.

Reverse Osmosis for Families with Young Children

Reverse osmosis is the only widely accessible home filtration technology capable of removing fluoride, lead, arsenic and PFAS compounds from drinking water. For families with babies and toddlers, RO-filtered water is the safest choice for formula preparation and cooking, eliminating any concern about trace contaminants in water used for young children.

A common concern is whether RO water lacks minerals. Pure Plus+ and Pure Premium systems include a post-filter remineralisation stage that adds calcium and magnesium back into the water after the RO membrane, producing water with a balanced mineral profile that is both safe and great-tasting for all ages.

Jean-Paul explains this process to every family during the installation walkthrough, so you fully understand what the system removes and what it retains. There are no confusing statistics or technical jargon — just a clear explanation of what is in your water, what is being removed, and what you will notice straight away in the taste and quality.

The HPF-3 Whole House System — Protecting Your Entire Home

The HPF-3 is Filters For You's flagship product and the most comprehensive solution for Sydney families. Installed at your mains water entry point — typically beside the water meter — the HPF-3 filters every drop of water entering your home before it reaches any tap, shower, bath or appliance.

This means:

  • Your children's bath water is filtered — removing chlorine that can irritate sensitive skin and eczema-prone children
  • Your shower water is filtered — reducing chlorine absorption through skin and inhalation during hot showers
  • Your kitchen cold tap is filtered for cooking, filling kettles and ice makers
  • Your dishwasher and washing machine run on filtered water, reducing chemical residue on dishes and clothing
  • Your hot water system, taps and fittings are protected from sediment and microplastics over the long term

Water Filtration for Baby Formula Preparation

Preparing baby formula with filtered water is one of the most searched topics Jean-Paul hears about from new parents in Sydney. The concern is legitimate: formula preparation involves dissolving powder into water that an infant will consume entirely, and any contaminants in that water — including chloramine, fluoride and trace heavy metals — are consumed in concentrated form relative to an infant's body weight.

Jean-Paul recommends a reverse osmosis system for families preparing baby formula. The Pure Plus+ or Pure Premium removes fluoride, chloramines, heavy metals and microplastics to produce the cleanest possible water for formula. The remineralisation post-filter ensures the water has the appropriate mineral balance for infant consumption.

If you are expecting or have recently welcomed a new addition to your family, Jean-Paul can typically have a system installed within one week of your enquiry — well before your newborn arrives and long before you need to start relying on formula. The Real Economics of Bottled Water vs a Home Filter

Bottled water is expensive. For a Sydney family of four who drinks the recommended two litres per person per day, staying hydrated on bottled water costs between $15 and $25 per week — or $780 to $1,300 per year. Over five years, that is $3,900 to $6,500 spent on water alone, before accounting for the environmental cost of single-use plastic.

A Pure Plus+ reverse osmosis system installed for $840 delivers filtered water at less than a cent per litre. Most Sydney families recoup the full cost of the system within 18 to 24 months — and continue saving every year thereafter.

The whole house HPF-3 system involves a higher upfront cost from $3,150 but also provides value that bottled water simply cannot: filtered shower and bath water, protection for appliances, and peace of mind that every water source in your home is treated. For families who have been spending money on both drinking water and skin-safe bath products, the net saving often comes sooner than expected.

Health Outcomes Sydney Families Notice After Installation

The improvements Jean-Paul hears most often from Sydney families after installing a water filter system are consistent and largely immediate:

  • Better tasting water: The most immediate change. Chloramine has a distinct chemical taste and odour that most people become accustomed to. Within the first day of using filtered water, the difference is stark — and children who previously refused to drink tap water often start drinking from the filtered tap without hesitation.
  • Improved skin and hair: Families who install the HPF-3 whole house system — or who combine an under sink filter with a shower filter — frequently report reduced skin dryness, less itching and improved hair condition within two to four weeks. For children with eczema, the benefit is often particularly noticeable.
  • Reduced bottled water purchases: Most families stop buying bottled water entirely within the first week of installation and notice the savings within the first month.
  • Confidence in what children are drinking: Particularly for families with babies and toddlers, the peace of mind that comes from knowing the water is properly filtered is a significant quality-of-life improvement that is hard to quantify financially.
  • Better coffee and tea: Chloramine in tap water suppresses the flavour complexity of specialty coffee and tea. Filtered water makes a measurable difference in the cup — a bonus that Jean-Paul points out at installation and that many households report noticing immediately.
