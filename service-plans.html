Why Servicing Matters

An Unserviced Filter Stops Working

Water filter cartridges have a finite capacity. Once they're saturated, contaminants pass straight through, and most systems give no warning when this happens.

Filter Lifespan: 6–12 Months
Standard filter cartridges last 6–12 months. After that, filtration performance degrades significantly. Annual servicing keeps your system working as designed.
RO Membranes: 2–3 Years
Reverse osmosis membranes typically last 2–3 years. A clogged or expired membrane reduces flow rate and allows contaminants through. Regular inspection prevents this.
Manufacturer Warranty Compliance
Most water filter system warranties require annual servicing to remain valid. Filters For You service visits are carried out by a licensed plumber using certified parts.
Bacteria Risk in Unserviced Systems
An old, wet filter cartridge can become a breeding ground for bacteria. Sanitisation during your annual service eliminates biofilm and keeps your water genuinely clean.

What Happens on a Service Visit

What's Included

Cartridge Replacement
Old filter cartridges removed and replaced with fresh WaterMark certified cartridges matched to your system.
System Flush
Full system flush clears any residual carbon fines or debris after cartridge replacement. Water runs clear before sign-off.
Leak & Fitting Check
All connections, fittings and tubing inspected for wear, leaks or potential failure. Issues flagged and addressed on the spot.
Pressure Check (Standard+)
System pressure confirmed within manufacturer specifications. Low pressure is a common sign of a failing pre-filter or membrane.
Sanitisation (Standard+)
System sanitised to remove any biofilm build-up. Critical for systems that haven't been serviced in over 12 months.
Service Report
Every visit includes a written service summary. Premium plans include a detailed water quality report with test results.
