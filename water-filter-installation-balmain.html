Old Pipes, Sediment and Balmain's Water Quality

Balmain's charm — its heritage homes — comes with a water quality consideration that many residents don't immediately connect with their tap water. Homes built before 1960 often have original or early-generation plumbing: galvanised steel pipes, lead-soldered copper joints, or cast iron drainage. As these materials age, they can leach trace metals and sediment directly into the cold water supply inside your home.

If you've ever noticed discoloured water after work on nearby mains, gritty sediment settling in your kettle, or an unusual metallic taste that Sydney's standard tap water doesn't quite explain — older pipework is a likely contributor. This is distinct from the quality of water leaving Sydney Water's treatment plants; the issue arises between the mains and your tap.

Water filtration systems address this problem at the point of use. A good under-sink carbon block filter removes sediment, chlorine and taste compounds. For households with genuine concerns about lead or trace metals — common in pre-1970 Balmain properties — a reverse osmosis system is the more thorough solution. RO membranes filter to 0.0001 micron, removing dissolved heavy metals, lead, nitrates and virtually all other contaminants before the water reaches your glass.

Even modern Balmain apartments benefit from filtration. Sydney Water treats the entire network with chlorine and chloramines, and chloramines in particular produce a persistent taste and odour that carbon filtration effectively removes. The result after installation is immediately noticeable — cleaner, fresher water from every filtered tap.
