Water Quality in Burwood — What You're Actually Drinking

Burwood residents are supplied by Sydney Water through the Canterbury distribution network. The water meets Australian Drinking Water Guidelines, but that's a regulatory floor — not a quality ceiling. Sydney Water treats the supply with chlorine and chloramines to control bacteria, and while effective for safety, these disinfectants create a noticeable taste and odour that many Burwood households find unpleasant.

Beyond the obvious chlorine taste, Sydney's water supply also contains:

  • Chloramines — secondary disinfectants with a distinctive swimming-pool-like taste, harder to remove than chlorine alone
  • Trihalomethanes (THMs) — disinfection byproducts linked to health concerns at high long-term exposure levels
  • Microplastics — tiny plastic particles detected in most urban water supplies globally
  • Fluoride — added for dental health, but some households prefer to filter it out
  • Sediment and pipe particles — particularly relevant in Burwood's mix of older and newer housing

A quality water filtration system addresses all of these before the water reaches your tap. For Burwood families, the most popular choice is an under-sink reverse osmosis system — it fits neatly under the kitchen sink, doesn't require any changes to main plumbing, and produces noticeably better water immediately after installation.

Burwood Houses: Under Sink or Whole House?

For Burwood homeowners with a house, there's a choice between point-of-use filtration (under-sink or RO) and whole-house filtration (HPF-3 at the mains).

Under-sink filtration is perfect if your main concern is drinking and cooking water. It's cost-effective, compact, and Jean-Paul can have it installed in under two hours. Most Burwood houses have easily accessible kitchen plumbing that makes this a straightforward job.

Whole house filtration makes more sense if you want to filter water at every outlet — showers, baths, garden taps and appliances included. Chlorine and chloramines are absorbed through skin during showering and inhaled as steam, so some homeowners prefer to filter everything at the entry point. The HPF-3 at $3,150 is a bigger investment but covers the entire home.

Jean-Paul will assess your home and give you an honest recommendation based on your priorities and budget — not just the most expensive option.
