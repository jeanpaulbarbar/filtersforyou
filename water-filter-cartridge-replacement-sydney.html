An overdue cartridge doesn't just perform poorly — it can make water worse

Once a carbon cartridge reaches capacity, it can begin releasing previously trapped chlorine, sediment and organic compounds back into your filtered water. Worse, a spent cartridge creates conditions for bacterial biofilm to develop inside the housing. Annual replacement is not optional maintenance — it's essential to keeping your filter safe and effective.

What Jean-Paul Replaces

Cartridge Types Explained

Different cartridges within your system have different service lives. Jean-Paul identifies exactly what's due and replaces only what's needed.

Replace Every 12 Months

Sediment Pre-Filter

The first stage in most systems. Removes fine particles, rust and sediment that would otherwise clog the carbon and membrane stages. When blocked, the entire system flow rate drops. Standard 5 or 1 micron polypropylene cartridge.

Replace Every 6–12 Months

Carbon Block Filter

The workhorse stage — removes chlorine, chloramines, organic compounds, taste and odour. Coconut shell activated carbon block is the most effective type. When exhausted, chlorine taste returns and organic contaminant removal drops. Replace every 6 months for families with high usage, 12 months for typical households.

Replace Every 2–3 Years

RO Membrane

The semi-permeable membrane in reverse osmosis systems. Removes fluoride, heavy metals, PFAS, dissolved solids and bacteria. Performance degrades gradually — a TDS meter reading confirms when replacement is due. Typically lasts 2–3 years depending on feed water quality and usage.

Replace Every 12 Months

Alkaline Post-Filter

Found in the Pure Advanced and Pure Compact systems. Adds beneficial minerals (calcium, magnesium) back to RO water to improve pH and taste. Media is exhausted after 12 months and the remineralisation benefit is lost. Contains calcite, magnesium oxide and other mineral media.

Replace Every 12 Months

Inline Carbon Post-Filter

A small inline cartridge found after the RO storage tank in most 5-stage and 7-stage systems. Provides a final taste polish to water drawn from the tank. Often overlooked during DIY servicing, but essential for clean-tasting RO water.

Replace Every 12 Months

Quick-Change Cartridges

Used in the Pure Advanced Quick-Change systems (FSA range). Tool-free twist-and-click replacement. Proprietary cartridge format — Jean-Paul stocks these and can replace them on the same visit without needing to order parts.

Signs Your Cartridges Are Due

How to Know When to Replace

Water tastes or smells like chlorine again

If your filtered water previously tasted clean and neutral but has started to taste or smell of chlorine, this is a clear sign the carbon cartridge has reached capacity. Do not delay — a spent carbon cartridge is no longer protecting you.

Noticeably reduced flow rate

A gradual drop in flow rate from the filtered tap is most commonly caused by a sediment cartridge that is becoming blocked. This is normal — the cartridge is doing its job — but when flow becomes noticeably slow it's time to replace. On RO systems, flow rate drop can also indicate a degraded membrane.

You've exceeded the service interval

The simplest and most reliable indicator. If it's been more than 12 months since your last cartridge replacement, schedule one now regardless of whether you've noticed any symptoms. Performance degrades gradually and may not be obvious until the cartridge is completely spent.

RO system filling slowly or not reaching full pressure

If your RO storage tank is taking significantly longer to refill than it used to, or the water pressure from the RO tap is notably weaker, the membrane may need replacing. A TDS (total dissolved solids) meter reading can confirm — if rejection rate has dropped below 85–90%, the membrane is due.

System hasn't been serviced since installation

Jean-Paul frequently visits systems that have never had a cartridge change since the original installation — sometimes years later. This is the most common avoidable issue he encounters. If you can't remember the last service, assume it's overdue and book one.
