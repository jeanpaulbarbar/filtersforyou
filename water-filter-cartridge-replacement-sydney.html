Why It Matters

Why Cartridge Replacement Matters

A water filter is only as good as its cartridges. Over time, every filter cartridge becomes saturated with the contaminants it has removed from your water. Once saturated, the cartridge loses effectiveness — and in some cases, can release contaminants back into your water. Regular replacement is not optional — it is how the system works.

The most common mistake Sydney homeowners make

Installing a water filter and then forgetting about it. A filter installed two years ago with no cartridge change is providing little to no filtration benefit. If you cannot remember the last time your filter was serviced, it is overdue.

Water tastes or smells different

If you notice chlorine taste or odour returning to your filtered water, your carbon cartridge is spent. Replace it promptly.

Reduced water pressure at the tap

A clogged sediment cartridge restricts water flow. Noticeably reduced pressure from your filter tap is a clear sign replacement is needed.

It has been more than 12 months

Even if nothing seems wrong, annual cartridge replacement is the standard. Saturation is not always visible or detectable by taste.

RO water production is slow

If your reverse osmosis tank is taking much longer to fill than usual, the pre-filters or membrane may need replacement.

System makes unusual sounds

Gurgling, hissing or unusual noises from your system can indicate pressure issues related to cartridge blockage or membrane degradation.

You have just moved into a property

If a water filter is installed in a property you are moving into, assume the cartridges need replacing. You have no way to know when they were last changed.

Know the Difference

DIY vs Professional Replacement

Some systems are designed for user-friendly cartridge replacement. Others require professional tools, technical knowledge or plumbing connections that should only be handled by a licensed plumber. Here is Jean-Paul's honest assessment.

When DIY replacement is suitable

  • Quick-change cartridge systems (twist-off design) — these are explicitly designed for user replacement
  • Simple inline cartridges with standard housings and a filter wrench
  • If the manufacturer's manual confirms the cartridge can be user-replaced
  • Jean-Paul will always show you how at installation and advise whether your system is DIY-friendly

When a professional is recommended

  • Any replacement that requires isolating the water supply or disconnecting plumbing connections
  • RO membrane replacement (requires pressure testing after replacement)
  • Whole house systems — typically require tools and knowledge of the system's pressure ratings
  • Any system where you are not confident — a cartridge fitted incorrectly can leak or allow unfiltered water to bypass the cartridge
  • Systems under manufacturer warranty — incorrect DIY replacement may void the warranty

An expired cartridge stops filtering effectively. In the best case, water quality simply declines back toward unfiltered levels. In worse cases, a clogged sediment cartridge can restrict flow or cause backpressure on the system, and a spent RO membrane may allow contaminants to pass through at elevated levels. Regular replacement protects both your water quality and your system's longevity. The investment in annual replacement is small compared to the cost of a system repair or replacement.
