Why Do Some Water Filter Installation Quotes Look Cheaper?

There are three common reasons a competitor's quote might look significantly cheaper than Filters For You's fixed prices — and all three represent a risk to you as a customer.

1. Unlicensed Labour

Any water filter installation that involves connecting to your cold water supply is classified as plumbing work under the Plumbing and Drainage Act 2011 (NSW). This work must be performed by a NSW Licensed Plumber. Unlicensed installation is illegal in NSW, voids your home and contents insurance (most policies exclude damage caused by unlicensed trades), and gives you no legal recourse if there is a leak or failure.

Jean-Paul Barbar holds NSW Plumbing Licence 461511C, which you can verify directly at NSW Fair Trading (fair.trading.nsw.gov.au). Always ask any installer for their licence number before booking.

2. System and Installation Quoted Separately

Many filter companies advertise system prices online without including installation. The advertised price looks low because it only covers the system — you then pay a separate installation fee, call-out fee and potentially a fitting fee on top. The final cost is often equal to or higher than Filters For You's all-inclusive price, but you only find out at invoice time.

3. Non-WaterMark Certified Systems

WaterMark certification is mandatory for plumbing products in Australia. A WaterMark-certified filter has been independently tested and approved for contact with drinking water supplies. Non-certified systems — often sourced cheaply from overseas — may use materials that leach contaminants into your water. All systems supplied by Filters For You carry WaterMark certification.

Is a Licensed Plumber Required to Install a Water Filter in NSW?

Yes. Any work involving connection to a water supply line — including the cold water line under your kitchen sink — is classified as plumbing work and requires a current NSW Plumbing Licence. This is mandated by the Plumbing and Drainage Act 2011 (NSW) and enforced by NSW Fair Trading.

Installing a water filter yourself, or using an unlicensed handyman, is illegal in NSW. Beyond the legal risk, unlicensed plumbing work typically voids the water damage clauses of home and contents insurance policies — meaning a small leak from a poorly installed filter fitting could result in a denied insurance claim for thousands of dollars of water damage.
