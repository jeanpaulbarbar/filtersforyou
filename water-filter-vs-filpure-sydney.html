Why the Licensing Issue Actually Matters for Sydney Homeowners

This is the point that most customers don't realise until something goes wrong. Under the NSW Plumbing and Drainage Act 2011, any plumbing work that connects to or affects the water supply — including under-sink water filter installation — must be carried out by a licensed plumber. Not a technician. Not a handyman. A licensed plumber.

Why does this matter practically?

  • Home insurance: Some home insurance policies require plumbing work to be performed by a licensed plumber. An installation by an unlicensed person could void your coverage in the event of a water leak.
  • Warranty: If a product failure causes water damage and the installation was carried out by an unlicensed person, the manufacturer may not honour the system warranty.
  • Quality assurance: A licensed plumber is required to carry out and certify work to Australian Standards. The licence number is accountable — it can be reported if the work is defective.

Jean-Paul's licence is NSW 461511C. It's publicly verifiable and comes with professional accountability that unlicensed installation simply cannot provide.
